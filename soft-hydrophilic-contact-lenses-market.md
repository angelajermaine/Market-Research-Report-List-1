<p><p>Soft (hydrophilic) contact lenses are a type of contact lens made from a material that can absorb and retain water, making it highly comfortable and allowing for increased oxygen permeability to the cornea. These lenses are flexible and conform to the shape of the eye, providing a secure fit and clear vision. They are widely used for correcting refractive errors, such as nearsightedness, farsightedness, and astigmatism.</p><p>The soft (hydrophilic) contact lenses market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. Factors driving this growth include the increasing prevalence of vision disorders, rising awareness about eye care, and advancements in lens technology. The growing aging population and lifestyle changes, such as increased screen time and exposure to digital devices, are also contributing to the demand for contact lenses.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is benefiting from the availability of a wide range of soft contact lens options, including daily disposable, monthly disposable, and extended wear lenses. These options provide convenience and flexibility for users, leading to increased adoption.</p><p>In terms of region, North America and Europe currently dominate the soft contact lenses market due to the high prevalence of refractive errors and the presence of a well-established healthcare infrastructure. Factors such as technological advancements, increasing demand for customized lenses, and expanding distribution channels are expected to further fuel market growth.</p></p>

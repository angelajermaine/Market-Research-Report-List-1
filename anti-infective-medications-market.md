<p><p>Anti-infective medications refer to drugs used to treat infections caused by microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, and fungi. Antivirals are medications specifically designed to inhibit the growth and replication of viruses, thus preventing viral infections. Antibacterials, also known as antibiotics, target and kill bacteria, thereby treating bacterial infections. Antifungals, on the other hand, are drugs used to combat fungal infections by inhibiting the growth and spread of fungi. These different types of anti-infective medications cater to specific types of infections, helping to eliminate or control microbial pathogens.</p></p>

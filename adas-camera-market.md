<p><p>ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems) camera market types include radar, LiDAR, ultrasonic, and others. Radar cameras use radio waves to detect objects and measure their distance and speed, making them suitable for collision avoidance and adaptive cruise control. LiDAR cameras work by emitting laser beams and measuring the time it takes for the beams to bounce back, enabling precise and detailed environment mapping for autonomous driving. Ultrasonic cameras use sound waves to detect objects, mainly benefiting parking assistance systems. Other types could include vision-based cameras that rely on image processing algorithms to identify objects and monitor driver behavior.</p></p>

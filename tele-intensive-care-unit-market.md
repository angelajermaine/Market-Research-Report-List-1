<p><p>A Tele-Intensive Care Unit (Tele-ICU) is a technology-enabled system that allows healthcare professionals to remotely monitor and manage critically ill patients in intensive care units (ICUs) from a centralized location. The market is driven by various factors, including the rising prevalence of chronic diseases, the increasing demand for remote patient monitoring, and the need to optimize critical care management. Additionally, the COVID-19 pandemic has further accelerated the adoption of Tele-ICU solutions as healthcare facilities strive to minimize physical contact and reduce the risk of healthcare-associated infections.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in technology, such as the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning algorithms, have enabled more accurate patient monitoring and predictive analytics, improving patient outcomes and reducing healthcare costs.

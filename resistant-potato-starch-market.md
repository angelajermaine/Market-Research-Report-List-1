<p><p>The resistant potato starch market consists of two main types: RS2 and RS3. RS2 is derived from raw potato starch and is resistant to digestion in the small intestine, making it a valuable dietary fiber. It has the ability to enhance the texture and viscosity of food products and is commonly used as a thickening agent in various food applications. On the other hand, RS3 is produced through a process called retrogradation, where cooked and cooled potato starch forms resistant crystalline structures. It is primarily used as a texturizer and stabilizer in the food industry.</p></p>

<p><p>Real-time PCR (QPCR) is a highly sensitive molecular technique used to quantify and analyze DNA or RNA in real time, allowing researchers to track the progression of polymerase chain reactions as they occur. Real-time PCR instruments and reagents are essential components of this technique.</p><p>Real-time PCR instruments are specialized machines that facilitate quantitative PCR analysis. They amplify and monitor the DNA or RNA in real time, providing accurate and precise measurements. These instruments are equipped with detectors that measure fluorescence emitted by the fluorescent dye used during PCR, enabling researchers to monitor the amplification process.</p><p>Real-time PCR reagents include primers, probes, and master mixes. Primers are short DNA sequences that hybridize to the target DNA, initiating the amplification process. Probes are fluorescently labeled DNA fragments that specifically bind to the target DNA, allowing researchers to measure the fluorescence signal during amplification. Master mixes contain all the necessary components for PCR, including enzymes, buffers, and dNTPs.</p><p>The Real-time PCR (QPCR) Instrument & Reagent Market is witnessing steady growth due to its increasing applications in various fields, including molecular diagnostics, gene expression analysis, genotyping, pathogen detection, and drug discovery. Additionally, the rising prevalence of infectious diseases, genetic disorders, and cancer fuels market growth.

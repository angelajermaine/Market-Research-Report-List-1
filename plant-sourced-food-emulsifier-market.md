<p><p>The plant-sourced food emulsifier market offers various types of emulsifiers. Mono-, di-glycerides, and their derivatives are widely used emulsifiers derived from vegetable oils. Lecithin, another commonly used emulsifier, is extracted from soybeans and acts as a natural emulsifying agent. Sorbitan esters are derived from sorbitol and have excellent stability and emulsifying properties. Stearoyl lactylates are obtained from plant-based sources and improve dough strength and emulsion stability. Polyglycerol esters are derived from plant oils and are used as emulsifiers and stabilizers in food products. Other plant-based emulsifiers may include polysorbates and acacia gum, among others.</p></p>

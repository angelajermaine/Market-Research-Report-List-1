<p><p>Scoliosis management market consists of two main types: pediatric and adult. Pediatric scoliosis refers to the condition that affects children and adolescents, usually developing during growth spurts. The management of pediatric scoliosis involves early detection, regular monitoring, and non-surgical interventions such as bracing and exercises. On the other hand, adult scoliosis occurs in individuals who have stopped growing and could be a result of previous childhood scoliosis or degenerative changes in the spine. Managing adult scoliosis often involves pain management, physical therapy, and occasionally surgery for severe cases.</p></p>

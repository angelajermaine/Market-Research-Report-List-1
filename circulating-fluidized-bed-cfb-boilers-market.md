<p><p>Circulating Fluidized Bed (CFB) boilers are a type of combustion technology used in power plants and industrial processes to burn various fuels such as coal, biomass, and waste materials. </p><p>CFB boilers operate by creating a high-velocity fluidized bed of material (usually sand or limestone) suspended within the furnace, which circulates the particles and enhances the combustion process. This technology offers several advantages, including improved combustion efficiency, reduced emissions of harmful pollutants such as sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxide, and the ability to burn a wide range of fuels.</p><p>The Circulating Fluidized Bed (CFB) Boilers market is expected to experience significant growth during the forecast period. <p><p>The circulating fluidized bed (CFB) boilers market can be categorized into three main types: subcritical, supercritical, and ultra-supercritical. Subcritical CFB boilers operate at low pressures and temperatures, providing an efficient and cost-effective solution. Supercritical CFB boilers operate at higher pressures and temperatures, allowing for higher efficiency and lower emissions. Ultra-supercritical CFB boilers operate at even higher pressures and temperatures, providing the highest levels of efficiency and lowest emissions. These different types cater to varying power plant requirements, providing options for improved performance and environmental sustainability.</p></p>

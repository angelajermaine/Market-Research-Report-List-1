<p><p>The electrolyzer market comprises two main types of electrolysers: traditional alkaline electrolyzers and PEM (Proton Exchange Membrane) electrolyzers. Traditional alkaline electrolyzers generate hydrogen gas by using a liquid alkaline electrolyte, typically potassium hydroxide, while PEM electrolyzers use a solid polymer membrane to facilitate the electrolysis process. Both types are used for hydrogen production, with traditional alkaline electrolyzers being commonly employed in large-scale industrial applications and PEM electrolyzers finding extensive use in smaller-scale and portable applications due to their compact size, high efficiency, and rapid response capabilities.</p></p>

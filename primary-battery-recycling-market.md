<p><p>Primary battery recycling refers to the process of collecting and reprocessing used batteries to recover valuable materials and reduce environmental pollution. There are two main types of primary battery recycling markets: wet and dry. Wet primary battery recycling focuses on recycling batteries that contain an electrolyte solution, such as lead-acid batteries commonly used in cars. Dry primary battery recycling involves the recycling of batteries that do not contain liquid electrolytes, typically including alkaline batteries commonly found in household devices. Both markets play a crucial role in minimizing the negative impact of battery waste on the environment.</p></p>

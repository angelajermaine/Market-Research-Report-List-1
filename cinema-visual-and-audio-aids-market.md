<p><p>The Cinema Visual and Audio Aids market comprises two main types: Assistive Listening Devices (ALDs) and Closed Captioning Devices (CCDs). ALDs help individuals with hearing impairments to better understand the audio in movies by providing amplified sound directly to their ears. These devices allow them to adjust the volume according to their specific needs. CCDs, on the other hand, display text or captions on a screen, enabling individuals with hearing disabilities to read the dialogue and understand the movie. Both ALDs and CCDs aim to make cinema experiences inclusive and accessible for all audiences.</p></p>

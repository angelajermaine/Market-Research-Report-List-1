<p><p>Mono audio codecs are used to convert audio signals into digital data and transmit or store them efficiently. In the analog market, codecs convert analog audio signals into continuous electrical signals for transmission through physical pathways like wires. In contrast, in the digital market, codecs convert audio signals into binary data, allowing them to be stored or transmitted in digital formats, such as MP3 or AAC files. Digital codecs offer advantages like better sound quality, higher fidelity, and greater flexibility, while analog codecs are simpler and more suitable for specific applications like old telephone systems.</p></p>

<p><p>High Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) is a versatile analytical technique used to separate, identify and quantify components in a mixture. It is widely used across various industries, including pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, food and beverage, environmental analysis, and forensic science. </p><p>HPLC works by passing a liquid sample through a column packed with stationary phase particles. The components present in the sample interact differently with the stationary phase, causing them to separate. Detection of the separated components is achieved through various techniques, such as UV/Vis spectroscopy or mass spectrometry.</p><p>The HPLC market has experienced significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue to expand. The increasing demand for reliable and accurate analysis of complex mixtures, advancements in technology, and the rising need for quality control in various industries are driving the market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the growing pharmaceutical and biotechnology sectors and increasing investment in research and development activities contribute to the market's growth. <p><strong>The High Performance Liquid Chromatography(HPLC) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Partition Chromatography</li><li>Normal-phase Chromatography</li><li>Displacement Chromatography</li><li>Reversed-phase Chromatography(RPC)</li><li>Size-exclusion Chromatography</li><li>Ion-exchange Chromatography</li><li>Bioaffinity Chromatography</li></ul></p>
<p><p>High Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) is a technique used to separate and analyze different components in a mixture. Partition Chromatography separates compounds based on their solubility, while Normal-phase Chromatography separates them based on polarity. Displacement Chromatography separates compounds by exploiting different affinities. Reversed-phase Chromatography (RPC) separates compounds based on their hydrophobicity. Size-exclusion Chromatography separates based on molecular size. Ion-exchange Chromatography separates compounds based on their charge. Bioaffinity Chromatography separates compounds based on specific biologically relevant interactions. All these techniques find applications in various industries for analytical and preparative purposes.</p></p>

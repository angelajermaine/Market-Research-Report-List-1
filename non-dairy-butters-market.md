<p><p>The non-dairy butters market can be classified into two types: spreadable and non-spreadable. Spreadable non-dairy butters have a softer texture and are designed to be easily spread on bread, toast, or other food items. They are typically made using oils and fats with a high melting point, allowing them to stay solid at room temperature but spread smoothly. Non-spreadable non-dairy butters, on the other hand, have a firmer texture and are primarily used for cooking and baking purposes. They are often made using oils and fats with a lower melting point, allowing them to melt and blend well into recipes.</p></p>

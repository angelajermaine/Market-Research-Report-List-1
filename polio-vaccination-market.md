<p><p>The polio vaccination market consists of two types of vaccines: Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV) and Inactivated Polio Vaccine (IPV). OPV is administered orally and contains weakened poliovirus strains to stimulate immunity. It is easy to administer and induces intestinal immunity, making it suitable for mass vaccination campaigns. IPV, on the other hand, is injected and contains inactivated poliovirus strains. It induces a strong immune response in the bloodstream, providing both intestinal and systemic immunity. Both vaccines play a crucial role in global efforts to eradicate polio and prevent its transmission.</p></p>

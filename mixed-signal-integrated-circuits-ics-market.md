<p><p>Mixed-signal integrated circuits (ICs) are electronic components that combine both digital and analog circuits on a single chip. Digital ICs process discrete and binary signals, representing information in the form of "ON" or "OFF" states. They are widely used in applications involving processing, storage, and transmission of digital data. On the other hand, analog ICs deal with continuous signals, processing fluctuations in amplitude, frequency, and phase. These ICs find applications in various fields like communication, audio/video processing, sensors, and power management systems. Both digital and analog ICs play crucial roles in modern electronic devices.</p></p>
